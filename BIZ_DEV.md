## 1. Go-To-Market Strategy

### Positioning

* Category creator: **Advisory-as-a-Service (AaaS)**.
* **LATAM-first**: builds on trust, consejo, and community-driven innovation.
* **Global scalability**: ROI-driven, structured governance for North America & Europe.

### Target Markets

1. **Internal MADFAM CABs** → validate features, refine playbooks, generate case studies.
2. **Innovation-driven SMEs (LATAM)** → first external cohort; community-oriented, price-sensitive.
3. **B2B SaaS / Manufacturing scale-ups (NA/EU)** → ROI- and integration-focused customers.
4. **Enterprise verticals** → expansion into financial services, healthcare, and government (post v3).

### ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

* Mid-size to enterprise B2B companies.
* Industries: SaaS, advanced manufacturing, cleantech, fintech.
* Needs: structured feedback loops, faster PMF, loyalty/retention boosts, referral-driven growth.
* Champions: Product leaders, Customer Success VPs, Innovation/Strategy heads.

---

## 2. Customer Acquisition Plan

### Early Traction (0–6 months)

* Run 2–3 **MADFAM internal CABs** to generate proof points.
* Target 5–10 external pilot clients in LATAM (warm intros, accelerators, VC networks).
* Offer **Founding Advisory Partner** status + steep discount (50–70%) for early adopters.

### Growth Stage (6–18 months)

* Publish **case studies & testimonials** from early CABs.
* Leverage **referral incentives**: CAB members → introduce 2–3 peers.
* Direct sales to LATAM mid-market; inbound campaigns in NA/EU.
* Conferences & partnerships: innovation hubs, chambers of commerce, industry events.

### Scale Stage (18–36 months)

* Verticalized playbooks (e.g., SaaS CAB-in-a-box, Manufacturing CAB-in-a-box).
* Launch **partner program** for consultants/facilitators.
* Co-sell with VCs/accelerators as “customer validation infrastructure” for their portfolios.

---

## 3. Pricing & Packaging

* **Starter (\$500–1k/mo):** 25 members, basic CRM, events.
* **Growth (\$2–3k/mo):** 100 members, integrations, advanced analytics.
* **Enterprise (\$5k+/mo):** unlimited members, SSO, API, SLA.
* **Add-ons:** facilitation services, insights reports, white-glove onboarding.

---

## 4. Partnerships & Channels

* **Accelerators/VCs:** Position Coforma Studio as a PMF validation tool.
* **Consultancies & Agencies:** Enable facilitation-as-a-service on top of the platform.
* **Integration Partners:** Jira, Asana, Slack, Zoom → cross-promotions & marketplace listings.
* **Regional Channels:** LATAM innovation hubs, US/EU SaaS associations, solarpunk/impact networks.

---

## 5. Marketing Strategy

* **Content Marketing:** Thought leadership on CABs, co-creation, and solarpunk innovation.
* **Community Building:** Webinars, roundtables, and a newsletter (*The Coforma Circle*).
* **Brand Narrative:** “Build with your customers, not just for them.”
* **Social:** LinkedIn + Twitter for B2B; Medium/blog for case studies.
* **PR:** Launch announcement, partnerships, customer success stories.

---

## 6. KPIs & Metrics

* # of internal MADFAM CABs active.
* # of external tenants onboarded.
* Referral-attributed pipeline (% of new business).
* Logo retention rate (gross & net).
* Average Revenue Per Account (ARPA).
* CAB engagement: attendance, feedback submitted, roadmap adoption.

---

## 7. Risks & Mitigation

* **Discount anchoring:** time-bound discounts with clear graduation paths.
* **Over-indexing on CAB feedback:** validate insights across broader segments.
* **Churn risk post-discount:** invest in ongoing value delivery (analytics, integrations).
* **Competitive entry:** differentiate as category creator (AaaS), not generic CRM/community.
